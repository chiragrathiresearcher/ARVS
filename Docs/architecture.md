# ARVS Architecture: Systems & Data Flow

This document provides a detailed technical overview of the **Adaptive Robust Verification System (ARVS)** architecture. ARVS is structured as a multi-layered, feedback-driven loop that integrates formal safety axioms with real-time probabilistic estimation and quantum-ready optimization.

---

## 1. High-Level Control Philosophy
ARVS treats verification not as a static pre-deployment check, but as a **continuous runtime process**. The system operates on a 10Hz control loop (defined in `constants.py`) that constantly balances mission objectives against quantified environmental and internal risks.



### Operational Modes
The system transitions through states based on the severity of detected faults and risk levels:
* **NORMAL**: Full performance; all axioms satisfied.
* **DEGRADED**: Minor fault detected or risk > 0.6. Performance is capped to preserve safety.
* **SAFE_HOLD**: High risk or minor axiom violation. The robot enters a known stable state.
* **EMERGENCY**: Critical axiom violation or hardware failure. Immediate power-down or hard-stop.

---

## 2. Functional Layer Decomposition

### A. Perception & Belief Layer
* **Modules**: `abstraction.py`, `feature_extractor.py`, `estimation.py`, `belief.py`
* **Responsibility**: Converts raw, noisy sensor observations into a high-confidence **Belief State**.
* **Key Feature**: Unlike standard systems, ARVS explicitly tracks **Uncertainty**. If sensor covariance grows too large, the `Belief` module triggers an Epistemic Axiom violation, forcing the robot to slow down until confidence is restored.

### B. Risk & Health Layer
* **Modules**: `quantification.py`, `detection.py`, `adaptive_models.py`
* **Responsibility**: Analyzes the Belief State across 8 dimensions of risk (Thermal, Structural, Power, etc.).
* **Key Feature**: **Adaptive Modeling**. If the physical behavior of the robot deviates from the expected model (e.g., a motor is dragging), `adaptive_models.py` updates the internal physics parameters to ensure the Safety Gate remains accurate.



### C. The Decision Engine (QUBO)
* **Modules**: `engine.py`, `mvi_logic.py`, `multi_robot.py`
* **Responsibility**: Solves the "Optimal Intervention" problem.
* **Key Feature**: **QUBO Formulation**. The system maps safety constraints and mission goals into a Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization (QUBO) matrix ($x^T Q x$). This allows the system to utilize classical Tabu search or offload to **Quantum Annealing** hardware for high-complexity swarm coordination.

### D. Enforcement & Execution
* **Modules**: `safety_gate.py`, `controller.py`
* **Responsibility**: The deterministic "Last Mile" of safety.
* **Key Feature**: **The Safety Gate**. Every action sequence generated by the AI/Optimizer must pass through a non-AI, rule-based filter. This ensures that even if the optimization produces a mathematical "optimum" that is physically dangerous, the Gate blocks it.

## 3. Reliability & Hardening (Space-Grade)
ARVS is designed for extreme environments where hardware reliability is not guaranteed.

1.  **Radiation Hardening (`math_utils.py`)**: Implements **Triple Modular Redundancy (TMR)**. Critical calculations are performed through three distinct paths to detect and correct "bit-flips" caused by cosmic radiation.
2.  **Temporal Integrity (`timing.py`)**: Utilizes a high-precision `SpacecraftClock` and Hardware Watchdogs to prevent "race conditions" and system freezes.
3.  **Tamper-Proof Auditing (`logger.py`)**: Every decision, risk score, and axiom violation is recorded in a cryptographically hashed (SHA-256) chain, ensuring a forensic-grade record for post-mission analysis.

## 4. Logical Data Flow Example
1.  **Detection**: `detection.py` senses an unexpected rise in `joint_torque`.
2.  **Logic Update**: `adaptive_models.py` adjusts the "Structural Load" threshold.
3.  **Risk Assessment**: `quantification.py` raises the `StructuralRisk` to 0.75.
4.  **Optimization**: `engine.py` generates a QUBO problem that prioritizes "Torque Reduction" over "Speed."
5.  **Validation**: `safety_gate.py` confirms the new path stays within the `constants.py` thermal limits.
6.  **Action**: `controller.py` executes the dampened movement.
